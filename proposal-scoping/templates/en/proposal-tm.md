# [Engagement name]

**Client:** Name and legal entity.
**Vendor:** Name and legal entity.
**Prepared by:** Name, role, date.
**Version:** Number and date.

## 1. Summary

Two to four sentences. What we are doing together, over what window, at what cadence. No pitch. No brand voice.

## 2. Commercial model

Time and materials.

- **Rate.** Hourly or daily rate, or a rate card by seniority.
- **Cadence.** Weekly or biweekly invoicing.
- **Cap.** Optional monthly cap. If present, name it. If absent, say so.

## 3. Engagement shape

- **Team.** Roles committed, named people if known, percentage allocation.
- **Working hours.** Time zone overlap required with the client.
- **Communication.** Channels and expected response times.
- **Cadence.** Standing meetings with purpose and length.

## 4. Work types included

What this team is allowed to work on under the engagement.

- Work type 1.
- Work type 2.

Anything not on this list triggers a scope conversation, not a ticket.

## 5. Work types excluded

What the engagement does not cover.

- Exclusion 1. Reason.
- Exclusion 2. Reason.

## 6. Dependencies

What the client provides, with owners and deadlines.

- Access to [system]. Owner. By date.
- Named decision maker available [cadence].
- Dependency 3. Owner. Deadline.

## 7. Reporting

- **Weekly update.** Format, audience, what it contains.
- **Monthly review.** Format, audience, what it contains.
- **Burndown visibility.** How the client sees hours used vs hours remaining.

## 8. Change management

One paragraph. What counts as a change, who requests and who approves, how long evaluation takes, how price or timeline is recalculated.

## 9. Kill clause

Notice period. What happens to work in progress. Intellectual property treatment. Treatment of already-paid fees.

## 10. Acceptance

Signature blocks. Names, titles, dates.
