# [Project or engagement name] update

> Routine delivery update for an external client. For a date that is going to slip, use `escalation-note` instead.

**To:** [Client name and role.]
**From:** [Your name and role. A single person, not a team.]
**Period:** [Date range this update covers.]
**Overall:** [On track / At risk / Slipping. One word, then the rest of the update earns it.]

---

## What shipped since the last update

What the client can now see, use, or rely on. Phrase as outcomes for them, not tasks closed.

- [You can now [outcome the client can observe].]
- [[Feature or deliverable] is live for [user or context].]
- [[Internal milestone that matters to the client], which means [why it matters to them].]

## On track

What you still expect to hit as planned. Keep it short. No hedging.

- [[Deliverable], still on track for [date].]
- [[Deliverable], still on track for [date].]

## At risk

What might not land as planned. Name each one plainly. Do not soften.

- [[Deliverable] is at risk because [reason]. [What you are doing about it.]]
- [[Deliverable] is at risk because [reason]. [What you are doing about it.]]

If nothing is at risk, write "Nothing at risk this period" rather than leaving it blank.

## Changes to scope or date

State each change and its reason in the same line. If nothing changed, write "No changes this period".

- [[What moved] changed from [old] to [new] because [reason].]

## What we need from you

Each ask with the specific date you need it by and what it blocks until then.

- [[Decision, approval, access, or contact] by [date]. Blocks [what stalls without it].]
- [[Decision, approval, access, or contact] by [date]. Blocks [what stalls without it].]

## Next milestone

One milestone and the date you are aiming for.

> [Next concrete milestone] on [date].
