# Philosophy

The spine of this repo. Every skill operationalizes one or more of the ten concepts below. Skills that touch product or process name the concepts they carry. Skills that are more mechanical carry a single anchor line. The through-line is consistent: method over voice, decisions over descriptions, structure over style.

Drawn from *Manual de ataraxia digital: imperturbabilidad entre tanto scrum* (Federico Imparatta, Montevideo, 2025).

---

## 1. Output vs outcomes

Output is what you do. Outcomes is what changes because of what you do. Teams that confuse them optimize for the visible over the valuable. Every artifact this repo produces is judged by the outcome it enables, not by how much of it there is.

*Used in: prd-authoring, feature-proposal, roadmap-from-data, analytics-instrumentation, post-mortem.*

## 2. Product as a portfolio of bets

Every decision is a bet under uncertainty. The problem is not betting. The problem is not knowing you are betting. Good teams bet often, but small, and distribute risk across the portfolio.

*Used in: feature-proposal, roadmap-from-data, prd-authoring.*

## 3. Reduce uncertainty, not scope

An MVP is a hypothesis, not a stripped-down version of the final product. Cutting features is not the same as cutting what distracts from the experiment. The first question is always: what do we need to learn, and what is the cheapest way to learn it.

*Used in: prd-authoring, feature-proposal, proposal-scoping.*

## 4. No heroes, no blame

If everything depends on one person saving the day, something is broken. Healthy management does not punish error but demands responsibility. Heroics usually hide structural problems that will surface again.

*Used in: post-mortem, candidate-evaluation, meeting-prep.*

## 5. Rituals serve results

Ceremonies are tools, not religion. A ritual that does not help alignment, decisions, or reflection is theater. The process serves the team, not the other way around.

*Used in: ticket-triage, meeting-prep, documentation-extraction.*

## 6. PM as anthropologist, not CEO

The product manager observes, connects, translates. Not a boss but a catalyst who moves between technical, commercial, and operational worlds and carries context between them.

*Used in: ux-audit, data-reconciliation, prd-authoring.*

## 7. Discovery is a practice, not a phase

Discovery is not a gate before execution. It is not an excuse to fill boards with sticky notes. Real discovery uses uncomfortable questions, behavior analysis, and validations that kill ideas you loved.

*Used in: ux-audit, prd-authoring, feature-proposal.*

## 8. Roadmap as conversation, not contract

Product does not predict, it learns. A roadmap is a prioritized view of what we want to learn, not an armored plan that punishes change.

*Used in: roadmap-from-data, proposal-scoping.*

## 9. Leading without title

Lateral influence over hierarchical authority. Slower, deeper. People follow because they want to, not because they have to.

*Used in: meeting-prep, post-mortem.*

## 10. Cultural signals are delivery

Cultural friction is not a soft skill. Ignoring it is expensive. Integration, alignment, and trust are work, not assumptions that follow from a signed contract.

*Used in: candidate-evaluation, vendor-evaluation, post-mortem.*
