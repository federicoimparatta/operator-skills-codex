# [Feature name]

> Lean PRD. Use at discovery or shaping stage. When the feature moves to build, migrate to `prd-full`.

**Owner:** Name and role. A single person, not a team.
**Status:** Draft, in review, approved, paused, killed.
**Last updated:** Date.

---

## Problem

Write a tight paragraph that answers:

- **Who feels it.** A specific user type, not "users" or "customers".
- **When they feel it.** A moment, a frequency, or a trigger.
- **What they do today.** The current workaround or the resignation.
- **What that costs.** Time, revenue, churn, support load, or a strategic gap.

If any of the four is missing, flag the gap rather than guess.

## Hypothesis

One sentence, in this shape:

> If we ship **X** for **user Y**, we expect **metric Z** to move by **roughly W**, because **reason R**.

## Evidence so far

Bullet list. What do we already know that supports the problem statement or the hypothesis. Link to interviews, dashboards, tickets, or prior experiments. Be explicit when a claim is inference rather than evidence.

## Scope

### In

What we commit to build now, phrased as outcomes users can notice.

### Out

What we are choosing not to build now, and why. This is the most important list.

### Not ever

What we believe is wrong for this feature, not just deprioritized.

## Success signal

The single metric or behavior change that would tell us the bet is paying off. Include the time window we expect to see it in.

## Open questions

List of open questions. Each with an owner and a deadline for resolution.

- Question 1. Owner. Deadline.
- Question 2. Owner. Deadline.

## Next step

One sentence. What happens next and who does it.
