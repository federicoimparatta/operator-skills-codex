# Feature proposal: [title]

**Author:** Name and role.
**Status:** Draft, in review, decided, parked, killed.
**Decision requested by:** Date or meeting.
**Last updated:** Date.

---

## 1. Problem

Three to five sentences. Who feels it. How it shows up today. What they do instead. Why it matters to the company.

## 2. The bet

One paragraph in this shape:

> We propose to invest **X effort** to test the hypothesis that **solution class Y** would move **metric Z** for **user W**. If the test shows **signal S**, we would commit to a full build.

## 3. User

One paragraph. Specific user type, segment size, and why they are the primary audience.

## 4. Evidence

Three to seven bullets. Mark each as **validated** or **inferred**.

- Evidence item. Validated or inferred.
- Evidence item. Validated or inferred.

## 5. Proposed direction

Two to four paragraphs at a conceptual level. What the user would do that they cannot do today. What changes in the product. What the team will not build.

## 6. Expected impact

- **Primary metric.** Name and current value.
- **Direction and magnitude.** Up or down, roughly by how much.
- **Time to move.** Weeks or quarters.
- **How measured.** Event, query, or dashboard that would show the move.
- **Second-order effects.** Positive and negative.

## 7. Validation plan

- **Method.** User research, prototype, experiment, dogfood, or combination.
- **Why this method.** What each alternative would fail to prove.
- **Cost.** Effort, cost in dollars, and calendar duration.
- **Audience.** Who is tested, from where, and how many.
- **Timeline.** Start and end dates.

## 8. Kill criteria

- Signal. Threshold. Source.
- Signal. Threshold. Source.

## 9. The ask

One paragraph.

- **Decision.** Approve validation, approve full build, park, kill.
- **By.** Date or meeting.
- **Blocker removed if approved.** What becomes possible.
- **Risk of delay.** What happens if this sits.

## 10. Open questions

Short list with owner and deadline.

- Question. Owner. Deadline.
